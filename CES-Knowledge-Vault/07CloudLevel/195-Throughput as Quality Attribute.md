## ğŸ“š Idea/Concept

Throughput is the effective rate of work completed per unit of time, measured under agreed validity and latency criteria. It differs from raw capacity (bandwidth) and is governed by Little's Law relating throughput, concurrency, and latency. As a quality attribute, throughput drives scaling decisions and must be validated through load testing.
