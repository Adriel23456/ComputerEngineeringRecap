The Scrum Master is a servant-leader responsible for promoting and supporting Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide. They help everyone understand Scrum theory, practices, rules, and values. The Scrum Master serves the Development Team, Product Owner, and Organization by facilitating Scrum events, removing impediments, coaching team members, and helping the organization adopt Scrum practices effectively.

### Core Competencies

Facilitation:
- Guides team interactions without directing
- Creates safe space for open communication
- Ensures all voices are heard

Coaching:
- Helps individuals and team improve
- Asks powerful questions
- Supports growth mindset

Teaching:
- Explains Scrum practices and values
- Shares knowledge and techniques
- Mentors team members

Impediment Removal:
- Identifies blockers proactively
- Escalates organizational impediments
- Removes obstacles to team progress

### Scrum Master Stances

| Stance | When Used |
|--------|-----------|
| Teacher | When team needs to learn Scrum |
| Coach | When team needs guidance to find solutions |
| Mentor | When sharing experience and wisdom |
| Facilitator | During events and team interactions |
| Servant | When removing impediments |
| Manager | When protecting the team |
| Change Agent | When driving organizational change |

### Common Anti-patterns

- Secretary: Only schedules meetings
- Police: Enforces rules rigidly
- Super-hero: Solves all problems alone
- Absent: Not present or engaged
- Admin: Only updates tools
