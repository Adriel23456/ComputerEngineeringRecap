## ğŸ“š Idea/Concept

Scrum is an agile framework for developing, delivering, and maintaining complex products. It defines a set of roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team), ceremonies (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective), and artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment) that work together in iterative cycles called Sprints. Scrum is based on empiricism and lean thinking, emphasizing transparency, inspection, and adaptation to manage complexity and uncertainty in product development.

### Three Pillars of Empiricism

Transparency:
- Significant aspects of the process must be visible to those responsible for the outcome
- Common language and shared understanding
- Definition of Done understood by all

Inspection:
- Scrum users must frequently inspect artifacts and progress
- Detect undesirable variances
- Not so frequent that inspection gets in the way of work

Adaptation:
- Adjust process or materials when deviations are detected
- Minimize further deviation
- Four formal events for inspection and adaptation

### Time-boxes

- Sprint: 1-4 weeks (typically 2 weeks)
- Sprint Planning: Max 8 hours for 4-week Sprint
- Daily Scrum: 15 minutes
- Sprint Review: Max 4 hours for 4-week Sprint
- Sprint Retrospective: Max 3 hours for 4-week Sprint
