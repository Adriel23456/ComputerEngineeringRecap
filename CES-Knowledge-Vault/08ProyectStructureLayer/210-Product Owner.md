## ğŸ“š Idea/Concept

The Product Owner is the Scrum role responsible for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Development Team. They are the only person responsible for managing the Product Backlog and serve as the bridge between the business, stakeholders, and the development team. The Product Owner has the authority to make decisions about the product, defines the vision, prioritizes work according to business value, and ensures the team understands the Product Backlog items. They must balance the needs of multiple stakeholders while maintaining a coherent product vision.

### Key Characteristics

Single Point of Authority:
- Only person who can manage the Product Backlog
- Final decisions about product content
- No one can contradict their decisions about priorities

Availability:
- Must be accessible to the team for clarifications
- Participates in Sprint Planning and Sprint Review
- Available during Sprint for questions

Value Focus:
- Every decision centers on maximizing business value
- Balances short-term needs with long-term vision
- ROI and metrics are their responsibility

### Product Owner vs Product Manager

| Aspect | Product Owner | Product Manager |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Focus | Delivery and backlog | Strategy and market |
| Scope | Single team/product | Product portfolio |
| Time horizon | Sprint to release | Quarters to years |
| Primary interaction | Dev team | Market and customers |

### Anti-patterns to Avoid

- Proxy PO: Someone who cannot make decisions
- Part-time PO: Divided attention reduces effectiveness
- Committee PO: Multiple people sharing the role
- Absent PO: Unavailable during Sprint
- Scribe PO: Only writes stories, doesn't own vision
