## üìö Idea/Concept

Computer architecture defines the interface between hardware and software, evolving through distinct generations driven by technological breakthroughs.

### Generational Timeline

First Generation (1946-1959):
- Vacuum tubes as switching elements
- Large, power-hungry, unreliable machines
- Examples: ENIAC, UNIVAC

Second Generation (1959-1965):
- Transistors replace vacuum tubes
- Smaller, faster, more reliable
- Introduction of high-level languages

Third Generation (1965-1971):
- Integrated circuits (ICs)
- Multiple transistors on single chip
- Operating systems emerge

Fourth Generation (1971-1980):
- VLSI technology (20,000 to 1,000,000 transistors)
- Microprocessors born
- Intel 4004 (1971): first commercial microprocessor (4-bit, 740 kHz)

Fifth Generation (1980-present):
- ULSI technology (more than 1,000,000 transistors)
- Multi-core processors (2005+)
- RISC-V project initiated (2010)

### Key Milestones
- 1958: Jack St. Clair invents the integrated circuit
- 1983-1991: GNU and Linux development
- 2005: First multi-core processors emerge

## üìå Key Points (Optional)
- Each generation brought orders of magnitude improvement in speed, size, and reliability.
- The transition from single-core to multi-core marks a fundamental shift in how we approach performance.
